My background

I've studied western astrology seriously since 2017, first in modern, psychological usage as written by Karen Hamaker-Zondag, Stephen Arroyo and Isabel Hickey. I wrote the Indiana Daily Student's weekly astrology column in my last semester of college when I had a fraction of the knowledge I do now.

I've since shifted gears to Hellenistic/traditional techniques: I use whole sign houses, traditional rulerships, the five classical aspects, and don't emphasize the outer planets or asteroids. This means that the chart I delineate doesn't include Uranus, Neptune or Pluto, and your house placements may differ from what you're used to if you've seen your chart before. I'll use them if they're relevant to your reading, but don't by default.

My approach

If you know me, I don't really perform mystical aesthetic—I own not a single crystal and only burn pumpkin spice candles. I understand astrology as a man-made system, just like language or religion, created in the context of a certain time to serve the interests of a certain class. It’s not dissimilar to how I think of Catholicism: a tradition with a (less violent) history you have to study before you break and remake it into something new.

I use astrology as a language to describe given circumstances, not determine outcomes. I think chart interpretation is less magic or counseling than it is translation. To me, the stars neither move us or amount to anything more prescient than objects assigned particular meanings. My practice is more concerned with external conditions than interiority or divinity, but I think the heavens, as given significance dating back to ancient Greece, mirror our personal and social dynamics, including our access to or restriction from resources.
