My background

I've studied western astrology seriously since 2017, first in modern usage while writing the Indiana Daily Student's weekly astrology column in my last semester of college with a fraction of the knowledge I have now. I've since shifted gears to Hellenistic/traditional techniques: I use whole sign houses, traditional rulerships, the five classical aspects, and don't emphasize the outer planets or asteroids. This means the chart I delineate might have different house placements than what you're used to if you've seen your chart before, and won't include Uranus, Neptune or Pluto by default.

I'm a student still, hence my rates! Buying a reading helps me learn. If this gives you pause, consulting astrologers I admire include Jo Gleason, Patrick Watson, Kelly Surtees and Amelia Quint.

My approach

If you know me, I don't really perform mysticism or think crystals do anything (but you do you!). I understand astrology as a man-made system, just like language or religion, created in the context of a certain time to serve the interests of a certain class. It’s not dissimilar to how I think of Catholicism: a tradition with a (less violent) history you have to study before you break and remake it into something new.

I use astrology as a language to describe given circumstances, not determine outcomes. Chart interpretation is less magic or counseling than it is translation. To me, the stars neither move us or amount to anything more prescient than objects assigned particular meanings. My practice is more concerned with external conditions than interiority or divinity, but I think the heavens, as given significance dating back to the Hellenistic period, are a means to model our personal and social dynamics, including our access to or restriction from resources.
