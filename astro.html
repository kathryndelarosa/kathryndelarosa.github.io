My background

I've studied western astrology seriously since 2017, with such fervor I amost failed out of journalism school while writing the Indiana Daily Student's weekly astrology column.

My approach

I draw from traditional techniques: I use whole sign houses, traditional rulerships, the five classical aspects, and don't emphasize the outer planets or asteroids. This means that the chart I delineate doesn't include Uranus, Neptune or Pluto, and your house placements may differ from what you're used to if you've seen your chart before.

While I wouldn't call myself an exlusively traditional or Hellenistic astrologer, I'm drawn to its tangibility. My practice is more concerned with external circumstances than interiority or divinity because astrology itself is a man-made system of meaning, so to me, "as above, so below" is material. The stars do not move us, but instead mirror our personal and social dynamics, including our access to or restriction from resources.
