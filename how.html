In tarot, the Temperance card is a balancing act. It’s that two extremes can coexist, have to coexist, to make a whole. Temperance is neither compromise or concession, but complements falling into step.

I really love Temperance. I relate to its contradictions, as a theater maker and political thinker. I’m American and not, I am both man and woman and mystical and rational, I have a Pisces sun and a Virgo moon, I conserve and abolish, beyond nationalism, but not without it. I like drama that is conventional and realistic and linear, with queer people of color and set anywhere but New York.

I think content is more radical than form, or at least the audiences I write to and about are challenged or alienated by content before form can budge. To acknowledge Suzan-Lori Parks: the figures which take up residence inside of me, at this point in my life, are comfortable in a linear structure—to date, my plays contain mostly white people because I write about isolation and assimilation.

I think we have more to learn about each other than we do about theater.

I write about history & how it haunts us, in elusive, ancestral pain & its materialist conception: how centuries of global capitalism have located these characters in their current conditions, however mundane or insular or provincial their daily lives seem. I'm interested in archive & memory.

I believe in specificity, from get the name of the dog to the narrative masterclass that is All Too Well by Taylor Swift.

I don’t believe in God because I believe in man.
And I love writing about Catholicism.

I speak in binaries & oxymorons and think of it like this: these two things are not the edges of possibility, but two points in an expanse. For Temperance to encompass two disparate ideas is to conjunct them, to heal a schism, to honor the past & preserve the good in a revolutionary future.

The theater I strive for is jubilant, and full of hope, because it's most of what I have. I come from resilience and survival, at the twice-colonized fringes of empire and in its very heart. I look at my home in Kentucky and see struggle & strength against oligarchy. I've lived two decades in the middle of America and wrenched it open to fit me and my contradictions. I take what has been forced on me and break and shape it to be mine. I have hope because we imagine and create compassionate, anarchic futures to care for ourselves and each other. But then again, I tattooed Uranus on my arm because it corresponds to The Fool.
