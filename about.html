Talambuhay

Kathryn de la Rosa (they/she) is a Filipinx playwright, dramaturg & audio producer from Kentucky.

They write plays about postcolonialism, diaspora, rurality, and belief, with humor and love for young women in the middle of America. The theater they strive for is at once radical and buoyant, and raises consciousness, honors the ordinary, and reaches for joy.

Kathryn was a 2019–20 dramaturgy apprentice at Actors Theatre of Louisville and previously interned with The New Harmony Project and Asolo Rep. Their plays have been developed with their Actors Theatre apprentice cohort, in IU Theatre’s At First Sight Festival, and by University Players. Kathryn holds a BA in theatre & drama and journalism from Indiana University, where they were the inaugural Indiana Daily Student astrologer and executive produced American Student Radio.

They did most of their growing up in Kentucky and Arkansas, and currently work remotely from unceded Seminole land on Florida's gulf coast.

Mini Manifesto:

Content can be radical independent of form, and we have more to learn about each other than we do about theater.
