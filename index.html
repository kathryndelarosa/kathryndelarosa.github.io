Kathryn de la Rosa is a writer, new play dramaturg and astrologer. Currently an MA student in Theology and the Arts at United Theological Seminary of the Twin Cities. Open to script consults, production & workshop dramaturgy, and birth chart readings.

Born at sunrise in Kentucky during a full moon, Kathryn is drawn to plays haunted by colonialism, Christ and capital, with all the contradictions they entail. Niches they've brought to collaborations range from historical materialism to journalism ethics to creating in-play tarot spreads. They've worked as a dramaturg at Actors Theatre of Louisville, The New Harmony Project, Working Title Playwrights, College of the Holy Cross, and the National Arts Diversity Integration Assocation.

Kathryn is part of the inaugural Ashland New Plays Festival New Voices cohort, and was a finalist for the first Artists at Play & APAFT Emerging Playwright Commission. Plays include Holy Virgins, NON SO PIÙ (i don't know what i am anymore), Ribs and The Best Day, and have been produced and developed by student groups at Northwestern University and Indiana University. Their MA thesis in progress is pasyon play, an epic Filipino answer to Sarah Ruhl with debts to Joma Sison, Eleazar Fernandez and Sir Anril P. Tiatco.
