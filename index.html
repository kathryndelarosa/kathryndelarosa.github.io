Kathryn de la Rosa is a writer, new play dramaturg and astrologer. Currently an MA student in Theology and the Arts at United Theological Seminary of the Twin Cities. Open to script consults, longer-term dramaturgy, and birth chart readings.

Born at sunrise in Kentucky during a full moon, Kathryn is drawn to plays haunted by colonialism, Christ and capital, with all the contradictions they entail. Kathryn is part of the first Ashland New Plays Festival New Voices Emerging Playwrights cohort, and was a finalist for the 2021 Artists at Play & APAFT Emerging Playwright Commission. Plays include NON SO PIÙ COSA SON, Holy Virgins, and Ribs, which have been produced and developed by student groups at Northwestern University and Indiana University. Their MA thesis in progress is pasyon play, an epic Filipino answer to Sarah Ruhl with debts to Joma Sison, Eleazar Fernandez and Sir Anril Pineda Tiatco.

Kathryn has worked as a dramaturg at Actors Theatre of Louisville, The New Harmony Project, Working Title Playwrights, Indiana University, and College of the Holy Cross. Niches they've brought to collaborations range from Vatican II to historical materialism to creating in-play tarot spreads. With their educational background in radio journalism, Kathryn has edited and produced podcasts for Actors Theatre of Louisville, Playing On Air, and Lantigua Williams & Co. They spent their formative years playing cello, singing art songs and hoarding Sondheim Tumblr URLs, so they love libretti and straight plays alike.
