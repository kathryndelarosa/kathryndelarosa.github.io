Kathryn de la Rosa—Writer, Dramaturg

Kathryn de la Rosa is a writer and new play dramaturg, born at sunrise in Kentucky during a Virgo full moon. They aspire to theater that is buoyantly radical, with love for people, their gods and their communities in the middle of America and the edges of its empire. Plays include Holy Virgins, about future communist queer Catholic school girls in Kentucky (Northwestern University, developed with 2019–20 Actors Theatre PTC); Ribs, a graduation love letter (Indiana University); The Best Day, for Taylor Swift on her 31st birthday; pasyon play, staging Christ's death and resurrection in Pampanga after the First Quarter Storm; and Joy & Joyce, on the Asian American liberal food media complex.

They've worked as a dramaturg at The New Harmony Project, Actors Theatre of Louisville, Working Title Playwrights, and on a one-on-one consulting basis for scripts and libretti alike. Niche skills & subjects they've used in dramaturgy range from creating in-universe tarot readings to Taylor Swift's discography. Starting in fall 2021, Kathryn plans to pursue graduate studies in theology, with interest in Philippine and Latin American liberation theology and liturgical drama. They write for Queer Kentucky and work as an audio editor and podcast producer.
