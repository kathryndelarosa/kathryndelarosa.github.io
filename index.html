Kathryn de la Rosa is a dramaturg & writer etc. from Kentucky's outer reaches, Prestonsburg + Paducah. Her interests span new plays, verse drama, and rural, immigrant & queer identities.

She is currently an apprentice at Actors Theatre of Louisville.

This year she earned her BA and BAJ from Indiana University Bloomington. She has worked on new plays and researched old ones for The New Harmony Project, Asolo Repertory Theatre and IU Theatre. As a playwright, her work has been or will be developed in the At First Sight Festival and by University Players.

A media jack-of-all-trades, Kathryn stumbled into sound design by way of radio production and is a self-taught front-end developer.

Kathryn is available for production dramaturgy, script consultation, writing, and anything audio. She's also trying to make tarot and astrology her side hustle.

■

She serves as production dramaturg for Little Women (ad. Chamberlain) dir. Jenny McKnight at IU Summer Theatre.

Kathryn assisted in a workshop of Donnetta Lavinia Grays' Where We Stand dir. Tamilla Woodard at The New Harmony Project. With Phaedra Scott, she lent dramaturgical support to writers-in-residence Jennifer Barclay and Robert Schenkkan.

For Granfalloon and IU Theatre, Kathryn played God in a staged reading of The Big Ten Theatre Consortium commission Bonnets (How Ladies of Good Breeding are Induced to Murder) by Jen Silverman.

Kathryn's play Ribs received a staged reading dir. Eleanor Sobczyk at IU.

In her final year at IU, Kathryn dramaturged VINEGAR TOM and HAUS OF MIRTH, sound designed TITUS ANDRONICUS and BARBECUE, and stage managed HIGH SCHOOL COVEN.

She capped her journalism degree with an astrology column in the Indiana Daily Student.

Her honors thesis was “The entertainment value of fact: Mike Daisey, ‘My Trip to Al-Qaeda’ and audience trust in docudrama.”
