Kathryn de la Rosa is a writer & dramaturg.

Born at sunrise in Kentucky during a full moon 🌝, they aspire to theater that is buoyantly radical, with love for people, their gods and their communities in the middle of America and the edges of its empire.

Plays include Holy Virgins, about future communist queer Catholic school girls in Kentucky (Northwestern University, developed with 2019–20 Actors Theatre PTC); pasyon play, staging Christ's death in Pampanga after the First Quarter Storm; Ribs, a graduation love letter (Indiana University); and The Best Day, for Taylor Swift on her 31st birthday. They have worked as a dramaturg at Actors Theatre of Louisville, The New Harmony Project, Working Title Playwrights, Indiana University, College of the Holy Cross, and directly with writers on stage and audio plays and libretti. Niches they've brought to collaborations range from Vatican II to journalism ethics to creating in-play tarot spreads.

In fall 2021, Kathryn plans to pursue an MA in theology, with interest in Filipino and Latin American liberation theology, postcolonial religious syncreticism, and theater about both. They majored in theatre & drama and journalism at Indiana University, where they wrote the inaugural Indiana Daily Student astrology column.
